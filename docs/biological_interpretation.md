# Biological Interpretation

## Overview

The integrated transcriptomic analyses demonstrated substantial molecular differences among breast cancer molecular subtypes.

Aggressive tumor groups, particularly HER2-positive and basal-like tumors, exhibited strong proliferative and mitotic transcriptomic signatures compared with normal breast tissue samples.

---

# Transcriptomic Heterogeneity

PCA and hierarchical clustering analyses revealed clear subtype-associated transcriptomic organization.

Key observations included:

- normal breast tissues formed highly distinct clusters
- HER2-positive tumors showed broad transcriptomic dispersion
- basal-like tumors exhibited increased molecular heterogeneity
- luminal subtypes demonstrated relatively compact clustering

These findings support the existence of subtype-specific molecular signatures in breast cancer.

---

# Biomarker Interpretation

Feature selection analysis identified several highly important transcriptomic biomarkers, including:

- PLK1
- CDC20
- TPX2
- BUB1
- ASPM
- TTK
- KIF14
- UBE2C

These genes are strongly associated with:

- mitotic progression
- spindle assembly
- chromosome segregation
- genomic instability
- tumor proliferation

---

# Differential Expression Analysis

Volcano plot analysis demonstrated extensive transcriptomic dysregulation between HER2-positive and normal breast tissue samples.

Significantly altered genes included:

## Upregulated Genes

- SCD5
- SCARA5
- TNXB/TNXA
- SH3GL3

## Downregulated Genes

- RRM2
- UBE2C
- UBE2T
- NUSAP1

The large number of significantly altered genes indicates substantial molecular reprogramming associated with HER2-positive breast cancer.

---

# GO Biological Process Interpretation

GO enrichment analysis demonstrated strong enrichment of pathways associated with:

- mitotic spindle checkpoint signaling
- chromosome segregation
- metaphase/anaphase transition
- mitotic nuclear division
- sister chromatid segregation

The enrichment of PLK1, CDC20, BUB1, TPX2, and TTK within these pathways indicates dysregulated mitotic checkpoint machinery in aggressive breast cancer subtypes.

---

# KEGG Pathway Interpretation

KEGG pathway analysis demonstrated significant enrichment of:

- Cell cycle
- Oocyte meiosis
- Ubiquitin-mediated proteolysis
- FOXO signaling pathway

These pathways are associated with:

- uncontrolled proliferation
- abnormal mitotic signaling
- genomic instability
- tumor progression

The strong enrichment of cell cycle pathways suggests increased proliferative activity within HER2-positive tumors.

---

# STRING Network Interpretation

STRING protein interaction analysis revealed highly interconnected proliferative signaling networks.

Major hub genes included:

- PLK1
- CDC20
- TPX2
- BUB1
- ASPM
- TTK

These genes formed dense mitotic interaction hubs associated with spindle checkpoint regulation and chromosome segregation.

The network connectivity supports the biological reliability of the identified biomarkers.

---

# Overall Biological Conclusion

The integrated analyses consistently demonstrated that aggressive breast cancer subtypes are strongly associated with dysregulated:

- mitotic checkpoint signaling
- spindle assembly
- chromosome segregation
- proliferative cell cycle pathways

The identified biomarkers may serve as potential subtype-associated molecular markers and possible therapeutic targets for future breast cancer studies.
