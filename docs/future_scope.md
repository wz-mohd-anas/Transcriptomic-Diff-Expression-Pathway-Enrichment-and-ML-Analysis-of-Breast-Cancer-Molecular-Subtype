# Future Scope

## Overview

This project successfully demonstrated the integration of transcriptomics, machine learning, and systems biology approaches for breast cancer molecular subtype analysis.

Several future improvements may further strengthen the biological and clinical significance of the study.

---

# External Dataset Validation

Future studies may validate the identified biomarkers using independent transcriptomic datasets from:

- GEO
- TCGA
- ArrayExpress

External validation would improve biomarker reliability and generalizability.

---

# Advanced Differential Expression Analysis

More advanced statistical transcriptomic analysis may be performed using:

- R/Bioconductor
- limma
- DESeq2
- edgeR

These tools may provide improved statistical modeling and transcriptomic interpretation.

---

# Survival Analysis

Future work may investigate:

- overall survival
- disease-free survival
- prognostic significance

of the identified biomarkers using survival analysis platforms such as:

- GEPIA
- Kaplan–Meier Plotter

---

# Gene Set Enrichment Analysis (GSEA)

Future studies may include:

- GSEA
- Reactome pathway analysis
- Hallmark pathway analysis

to further investigate subtype-associated biological mechanisms.

---

# Multi-Omics Integration

The project may be expanded using:

- proteomics
- methylation data
- metabolomics
- mutation profiles

to construct integrated multi-omics cancer models.

---

# Deep Learning Approaches

Future machine learning improvements may include:

- deep neural networks
- autoencoders
- convolutional neural networks
- graph neural networks

for improved subtype prediction and biomarker discovery.

---

# Drug Target Prediction

The identified mitotic biomarkers may be further explored for:

- therapeutic targeting
- drug repurposing
- precision oncology applications

Genes such as PLK1, CDC20, BUB1, and TTK may represent potential therapeutic targets for aggressive breast cancer subtypes.

---

# Personalized Medicine Applications

Future studies may investigate subtype-specific transcriptomic biomarkers for:

- personalized treatment strategies
- targeted therapy selection
- patient stratification
- precision oncology workflows

---

# Final Perspective

This project provided a strong foundation in transcriptomics, machine learning, bioinformatics, and systems biology analysis.

Future improvements integrating larger datasets, advanced statistical methods, and multi-omics approaches may substantially enhance the biological and clinical relevance of the findings.
